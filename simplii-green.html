Questions, answered

Everything people ask about spirulina.

Straight answers about taste, nutrition, fresh vs. powder and where to find SimpliiGood — with the numbers from our own label.

Does spirulina have to taste fishy?

No. The fishy, swampy taste comes from drying spirulina into powder. Fresh-frozen spirulina is flavor-free — SimpliiGood cubes are flash-frozen within 24 hours of harvest instead of dried, so they add the nutrition of spirulina to a smoothie without changing how it tastes.

Is fresh spirulina better than spirulina powder?

Fresh-frozen spirulina skips the heat-drying that powder goes through, so heat-sensitive compounds like phycocyanin and chlorophyll stay intact — and it has none of powder's fishy taste. Powder wins on shelf life; fresh-frozen wins on flavor and freshness. It lives in your freezer and drops straight into the blender.

What is the best way to take spirulina daily?

The easiest way is a fresh-frozen cube dropped straight into your blender — no measuring, no powder mess. SimpliiGood's serving is 2 cubes (28 g) blended into a smoothie, juice or bowl. Because the cubes are flavor-free, they disappear into anything from fruit smoothies to yogurt.

How much spirulina should I take per day?

SimpliiGood's labeled serving is 2 cubes (28 g of fresh spirulina) per day, blended into any drink or meal. If you're new to spirulina, starting with one cube is common. As with any new food or supplement, check with your clinician if you have a medical condition.

What is the nutrition in fresh spirulina cubes?

A 2-cube serving (28 g) of SimpliiGood fresh-frozen spirulina has 10 calories, 2 g of complete plant protein, 1.9 mg iron (10% DV), 60 mg potassium (2% DV), 8 mg vitamin C (9% DV), 1 g fiber, 0 g fat and 0 g sugar. A full 10-cube tray delivers 11 g protein and 50% DV of iron.

Is spirulina a complete protein?

Yes. Spirulina is a complete plant protein — it contains all nine essential amino acids your body can't make on its own. A 2-cube serving of SimpliiGood has 2 g of protein and a full 10-cube tray has 11 g, alongside iron, potassium and vitamin C.

How is SimpliiGood spirulina grown? Is it safe?

SimpliiGood spirulina is grown in greenhouse pools of food-grade fresh water, harvested daily at peak density, and flash-frozen within 24 hours. It is Non-GMO Project Verified, vegan and gluten-free, and the ingredient list is one line: spirulina. No fillers, binders, sweeteners or preservatives.

Do spirulina cubes need to stay frozen?

Yes — keep the tray in your freezer, like frozen fruit. The cubes ship cold and go into the blender straight from frozen; there's no need to thaw. Freezing is what preserves the fresh taste, color and nutrients that drying destroys.

Can you taste spirulina in a smoothie?

Not with fresh-frozen cubes. Dried spirulina powder adds a deep-green color and a swampy flavor many people reject; fresh-frozen spirulina adds the color and the nutrition with virtually no taste. Your smoothie tastes like the fruit in it — not like algae.

What is phycocyanin?

Phycocyanin is the blue-green antioxidant pigment that's unique to spirulina — it's one of the main reasons people take it. It is heat-sensitive, so much of it degrades when spirulina is dried into powder. Flash-freezing fresh spirulina keeps phycocyanin (and chlorophyll) intact.

Is spirulina vegan and gluten-free?

Yes. SimpliiGood fresh-frozen spirulina is Non-GMO Project Verified, vegan, gluten-free and kosher-style single-ingredient food: spirulina and water, nothing else. It has 0 g fat, 0 g sugar and no additives of any kind.

SimpliiGood is fresh spirulina that's flash-frozen within 24 hours of harvest — never dried, never powdered. That means no fishy taste, intact phycocyanin and chlorophyll, and zero prep: drop a cube in the blender. It's sold frozen in 300+ US stores and online, and lives in your freezer instead of a jar.
